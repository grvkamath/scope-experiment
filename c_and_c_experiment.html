We're conducting an experiment to see whether language AI systems produce natural-sounding continuations to certain kinds of phrases and sentences. In order to make such a judgment we need to first establish a baseline of what are and aren't natural-sounding continuations, using human assessments. This is where you, the experiment-taker, come in. You will read some sentences, along with possible continuations. Your task is to give your opinion on how natural-sounding each continuation is.

The experiment will take approximately 15 minutes. Read and agree to the consent form below, and we'll begin with two examples.


CONSENT FORM

You are invited to participate in a research study looking at the cases of language that do and don't sound natural. Please read this form carefully and ask any questions you may have before agreeing to take part in the study.

Investigators: Dr. Siva Reddy (Assistant Professor of Linguistics and Computer Science at McGill University) and Dr. Michael Wagner (Professor of Linguistics at McGill University), and students and research assistants under the two professors.

What this study is about: The purpose of this study is to investigate whether language AI systems are able to capture certain semantic structures present in natural language that humans are able to work with. This is investigated in terms of the types of continuations that language AI systems produce to certain types of phrases, and how these compare to human judgments of these continuations.
